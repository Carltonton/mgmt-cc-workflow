# Management Theory Building Standards

**Applies when:** Using `mgmt-theory-builder` skill or developing hypotheses/propositions for management research.

## Hypothesis Development: 3-Paragraph Blueprint

Every hypothesis or proposition must follow this structure:

**Paragraph 1 — Setup & Alignment:**
State the expected relationship and connect it to the overarching theoretical lens.

**Paragraph 2 — Logical Deduction:**
Step-by-step causal reasoning with transitional phrases. Explain "why" and "how" (A → M → B chain), not just "that" (A → B).

**Paragraph 3 — Synthesis & Formal Statement:**
Indented, bolded proposition starting with "Therefore, we propose/expect that..."

## Moderation Format

"Moderator moderates the relationship between A and B, such that the relationship is [stronger/weaker] when Moderator is [high/low] rather than [low/high]."

## Quality Gates

- [ ] All propositions/hypotheses are **testable** (operationalizable)
- [ ] Every construct has a **distinct definition** with established boundaries
- [ ] Mechanisms **align** with the overarching theoretical lens
- [ ] **Alternative explanations** are preemptively addressed
- [ ] Boundary conditions specify **contrasting scenarios** (high vs. low moderator)
- [ ] General constructs are **contextualized** to the specific research setting

## Theory Gap Identification Sources

- Contradictory findings across studies
- Under-explored constructs in existing frameworks
- Explanatory gaps in causal mechanisms
- Unclear boundary conditions

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Claiming "A affects B" without explaining the mechanism
- Using multiple theoretical lenses without integrating them
- Stating "based on X theory" without showing how the theory specifically generates the prediction
- Omitting boundary conditions or treating them as afterthoughts
