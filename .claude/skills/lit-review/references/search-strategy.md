# Literature Search Strategy

## Databases for Management Research

| Database | Coverage | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Web of Science** | Broad, high-impact journals | Citation analysis, systematic reviews |
| **Scopus** | Largest abstract database | Comprehensive search, coverage |
| **Business Source Complete** | Business & management | Full-text access, industry sources |
| **PsycINFO** | Psychology & behavioral sciences | OB, HR, psychology-related |
| **SSRN** | Preprints, working papers | Latest research, early access |
| **Google Scholar** | Broadest coverage | Finding missing sources, citation tracking |

## Boolean Operators

```
"exact phrase" - exact match
concept1 AND concept2 - both terms
concept1 OR concept2 - either term
concept1 NOT concept2 - exclude
concept* - wildcard
```

## Building Your Search String

**Step 1**: Identify key concepts
- Concept 1: Leadership
- Concept 2: Ethics
- Concept 3: Organizational outcomes

**Step 2**: Brainstorm synonyms/related terms
- Leadership: leader, management, supervision, managerial
- Ethics: ethical, moral, values, integrity, responsibility
- Outcomes: performance, effectiveness, success, results

**Step 3**: Combine with Boolean operators

```
(leadership OR leader* OR management OR supervis*) AND
(ethic* OR moral* OR values OR integrity OR responsibility) AND
(performance OR outcome* OR effective* OR success)
```

## Snowballing Technique

1. **Forward Snowballing**: Find articles that cited your key papers
2. **Backward Snowballing**: Check the reference lists of key papers
3. **Author Snowballing**: Find other works by key authors

## Search Scope Guidelines

- Prioritize recent work (last 5-10 years) unless seminal papers are older
- Note working papers vs. published papers — working papers may change
- Target: 15-25 citations for a 2-3 page introduction
- Restrict to ABS 4* journals for core positioning
