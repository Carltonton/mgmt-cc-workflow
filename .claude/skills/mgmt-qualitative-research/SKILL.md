# Management Qualitative Research

Guidance for designing and conducting qualitative research in management and business, with a focus on grounded theory, case studies, interview research, and thematic analysis.

## When This Skill Applies

- Planning a qualitative study
- Conducting interviews or focus groups
- Doing case study research
- Applying grounded theory methodology
- Coding and analyzing qualitative data
- Reporting qualitative findings
- Assessing rigor and credibility

## Qualitative Research Approaches

| Approach | When to Use | Key Features |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Grounded Theory** | Building new theory from data | Iterative coding, theoretical sampling, constant comparison |
| **Case Study** | In-depth examination of phenomenon | Rich description, multiple sources, bounded system |
| **Phenomenology** | Understanding lived experience | Bracketing, focus on meaning, in-depth interviews |
| **Ethnography** | Understanding cultural contexts | Participant observation, extended immersion |
| **Content Analysis** | Analyzing texts/media | Systematic coding, quantification possible |
| **Narrative Analysis** | Understanding stories | Focus on narrative structure, temporal sequence |

## Grounded Theory: Overview

Classic grounded theory (Glaser & Strauss, 1967; Flenady et al., 2025) follows 7 phases:

1. **Problem Identification & Theoretical Sensitivity** — Identify substantive area; limited literature review (methods only, not theory)
2. **Data Collection** — Interviews, observations, documents, secondary data; theoretical/purposeful/snowball sampling
3. **Coding** — Open → Axial → Selective coding with constant comparison
4. **Constant Comparison** — Compare incidents, codes, and categories iteratively
5. **Memoing** — Record theoretical insights throughout (code notes, theoretical notes, operational notes)
6. **Theoretical Sampling** — Sample based on emerging theory until saturation
7. **Theory Integration** — Write the narrative, create visual model, delimit boundaries

→ See `references/grounded-theory.md` for full phase details, coding examples, and memoing guidance.

## Self-Evaluation Checklist

- [ ] Research questions appropriate for qualitative methods?
- [ ] Research approach justified?
- [ ] Participants selected purposefully?
- [ ] Data collection documented thoroughly?
- [ ] Coding systematic and transparent?
- [ ] Multiple data sources used (triangulation)?
- [ ] Theoretical saturation demonstrated?
- [ ] Credibility strategies described?
- [ ] Quotes used appropriately as evidence?
- [ ] Contribution clearly articulated?
