# Writing Up Qualitative Research

## Writing Structure

1. **Introduction**
   - Research context and importance
   - Research questions
   - Brief overview of methodology

2. **Literature Review** (for grounded theory: methods-focused only)
   - Relevant theoretical background
   - Gap in knowledge

3. **Methodology**
   - Research design and justification
   - Participant characteristics
   - Data collection procedures
   - Data analysis procedures
   - Rigor and credibility strategies

4. **Findings**
   - Present themes/categories
   - Use quotes as evidence
   - Include visual models/diagrams
   - Tell the story of the findings

5. **Discussion**
   - Connect to theory
   - Discuss contributions
   - Limitations
   - Implications for practice
   - Future research directions

### Presenting Findings
- Use vivid quotes that exemplify each theme
- Balance quotes with analysis
- Don't just report—interpret and connect
- Use tables to organize themes
- Include figures to show relationships

## Rigor and Quality

| Credibility Standard | Definition | Strategies |
|---------------------|------------|------------|
| **Credibility** | Confidence in truth of findings | Prolonged engagement, member checking, peer debriefing |
| **Transferability** | Applicability to other contexts | Thick description, purposive sampling |
| **Dependability** | Consistency of findings | Audit trail, code-recode strategy |
| **Confirmability** | Objectivity of findings | Reflexivity, triangulation |

| 3-6 | Data collection (ongoing), open coding, initial memos |
| 7-8 | Axial coding, constant comparison, theoretical sampling |
| 9-10 | Selective coding, theory integration, model development |
